Hi, I'm Meenu Shevetha S, Founder of Sust.Enhance, Clinical & Sports Nutritionist, Certified Diabetes Educator (India), and NASM Certified Health Coach (USA).

Over the years, I've worked across government hospitals, private healthcare, sports academies, gyms, and nutrition clinics, helping over 1,000 individuals from every walk of life reclaim their health. Every setting taught me something different. But one lesson has stayed with me through all of it:

No two bodies are the same. So no two nutrition journeys should be either.

I've watched too many people trapped in the cycle of restrictive diets, short-lived results, and quiet self-blame when the plan stopped working. But the truth is simpler than "try harder": most diets ignore hormones, medical history, culture, lifestyle, and emotion, and then blame the person for not fitting the plan.

So my work starts differently. I don't hand you a meal plan and ask for discipline. I listen to what your body is trying to communicate, connect the dots, and build something that works with you, not against you.

I built the science behind that approach through a B.Sc. in Clinical Nutrition, an M.Sc. in Sports Nutrition (Gold Medalist), and certifications as a Certified Diabetes Educator and NASM Health Coach. But if my clients have taught me anything, it's this:

Credentials build knowledge. Listening builds transformation.

My clients come from very different lives: software professionals managing PCOS on impossible schedules, new mothers rebuilding strength after childbirth, athletes chasing peak performance, people who just want to enjoy food without guilt again. Different stories, but almost every consultation opens with the same sentence: "I'm tired of generic advice that doesn't fit my life." That's exactly where my work begins.

I don't believe healing means giving up the food you grew up loving. I don't believe health should feel like punishment. Nutrition should respect your culture, your schedule, your preferences, and your goals, because sustainable health is built through consistency, not restriction.

If you want someone to simply tell you what to eat, I'm probably not the right fit. But if you want someone to help you understand why your body feels the way it does, and guide you there with science, empathy, and honesty. I'd be honoured to walk that road with you.
