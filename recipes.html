Weight Management

High-Protein Greek Yogurt Bowl

5 min  ·  1 serving

A filling, high-protein breakfast that keeps hunger away for hours, no cooking required.

Ingredients & Instructions

Ingredients

  • 1 cup plain Greek yogurt
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter
  • 1/2 banana, sliced
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • Cinnamon, to taste

Instructions

  1. Spoon the yogurt into a bowl.
  2. Swirl in the peanut butter.
  3. Top with banana slices, chia seeds, and a dash of cinnamon.
Sports Nutrition

Post-Workout Chicken & Rice Bowl

25 min  ·  2 servings

A balanced carb-and-protein bowl to refuel after training, with vegetables for micronutrients.

Ingredients & Instructions

Ingredients

  • 200g chicken breast, diced
  • 1 cup cooked rice
  • 1 cup steamed broccoli
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt, pepper, garlic powder, to taste

Instructions

  1. Season the chicken and cook in olive oil over medium heat until browned through, about 8–10 minutes.
  2. Serve over rice with steamed broccoli on the side.

Low-GI Chickpea & Spinach Curry

30 min  ·  3 servings

A fibre-rich, low-glycemic-index curry that pairs well with insulin-sensitivity goals.

Ingredients & Instructions

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chickpeas
  • 2 cups spinach, chopped
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 tomatoes, pureed
  • 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tbsp oil

Instructions

  1. Sauté the onion in oil until golden, then add the spices and cook for 1 minute.
  2. Add the tomato puree and simmer for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the chickpeas and 1/2 cup water, simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the spinach and cook until wilted, about 2–3 minutes.

One-Pan Salmon & Veggies

25 min  ·  4 servings

An omega-3-rich, one-pan dinner that's easy to portion for the whole family.

Ingredients & Instructions

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets
  • 2 cups baby potatoes, halved
  • 1 cup green beans
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 lemon, sliced
  • Salt, pepper, dried herbs, to taste

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Toss the potatoes in half the oil and roast for 15 minutes on a tray.
  3. Add the salmon, green beans, and lemon slices to the tray; drizzle with remaining oil and season.
  4. Roast for another 12–15 minutes until the salmon is cooked through.

Diabetic-Friendly Vegetable Dalia

20 min  ·  2 servings

A high-fibre broken wheat upma loaded with vegetables, a low-GI Indian breakfast that's gentle on blood sugar.

Ingredients & Instructions

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup broken wheat (dalia), rinsed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas), diced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp mustard seeds, a few curry leaves
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 1.5 cups water, salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Dry roast the dalia lightly until fragrant, set aside.
  2. Heat oil, splutter mustard seeds and curry leaves, then sauté the onion until soft.
  3. Add the vegetables and cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
  4. Add the dalia, water, and salt, simmer covered for 10 to 12 minutes until soft.

PCOS-Friendly Ragi Dosa

15 min  ·  2 servings

A finger millet dosa with a low glycemic index, higher in fibre and calcium than a rice-only dosa, supportive of insulin sensitivity.

Ingredients & Instructions

Ingredients

  • 1 cup ragi (finger millet) flour
  • 1/4 cup rice flour
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chilli, finely chopped (optional)
  • Salt to taste, water as needed
  • 1 tsp oil, for cooking

Instructions

  1. Whisk the ragi flour, rice flour, onion, chilli, and salt with water into a thin, pourable batter.
  2. Rest the batter for 10 minutes.
  3. Pour a ladle onto a hot, lightly oiled pan and spread thin.
  4. Cook until the edges lift and the base is golden, flip briefly, and serve hot.
